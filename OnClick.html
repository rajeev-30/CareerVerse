    Bachelor of Technology (BTech) is a professional undergraduate engineering degree programme awarded to candidates after they complete four years of study in the field. Engineering is one of the most popular courses in India and there are many institutes that offer the course to aspiring students.

    For admissions, the most common BTech entrance examinations are JEE Main and JEE Advanced. Along with these national level entrance examinations, there are many state and private level entrance examinations that the students can attempt for admission to the course. The basic eligibility criteria for BTech is class 12 with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics. However, there are additional criteria in every entrance exam and institute. Some of the institutes also conduct admission to their courses on merit basis i.e. based on marks scored by candidates in their class 12 board exams.

Students can also apply for a distance BTech program online through the official website of the university or institute. For offline mode admission in BTech courses in distance learning, students can send the application form along with important verified documents to the regional center of the university.



    BTech Entrance Exams

    The most recommended entrance exam for BTech is the Joint Entrance Exam (JEE). There are two exams, namely: JEE Mains and JEE Advanced. This is a national level entrance exam that any candidate aspiring to obtain admissions to the top colleges in India should take. Here, in JEE Main, there are two papers and the candidates can appear for both the papers or either of the papers. The questions are formulated in a multiple-choice pattern. After clearing the JEE Mains, students seeking admissions into top colleges in India must attend the JEE Advanced.

    There are other state-level or college-level entrance examinations like the Vellore Institute of Technology Engineering Entrance Examination (VITEEE), Birla Institute of Technology and Science Admission Test (BITSAT), SRM Joint Engineering Entrance Examination (SRMJEE), etc. These exams vary in terms of the pattern, the cut-off, etc.

    The basic structure of all these entrance examinations is that the minimum required questions have to be answered by each candidate. The cut-off scores are determined by each authoritarian body conducting these examinations. A candidate who has secured the cut-off score would be able to take admission into the desired college after various other processes like the interview, written test, etc.

    The eligibility criteria for each college depends on the target set by the colleges or universities. The level of difficulty of each exam varies from the other. The assessment process also varies from one college to the other. The subjects covered in these exams include Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. The preparation should be such that all the important topics for the examination are covered. Candidates could also refer to the question paper pattern of the previous years or other samples to overview the types of questions asked in the examination.

    In India, all technical programs such as BTech, fall under the regulatory purview of the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) and the

    National Board of Accreditation (NBA). Admissions to B.Tech programs take place through national level/state-level/institute-level engineering entrance examinations. The top five engineering entrance exams taken by a large number of students each year are:

  • JEE Main-Conducted by National Testing Agency, JEE Main is the entrance examination for BTech admissions into NITs, IIITs and GFTIs. JEE Main consists of two papers - Paper 1 (BE/BTech) and Paper 2 (B.Arch/B.Plan)

  • JEE Advanced-It is a national level entrance examination for BTech admissions into IITs. Students have to qualify for JEE Main to attempt JEE Advanced.

  • VITEEE-One of the most popular private institute entrance examinations, VITEEE offers admissions into the BTech courses offered by Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT) and its various campuses.

  • MHT CET-Through MHT CET, students can apply for BTech admissions into the BTech institutes of the Maharashtra state.

  • TS EAMCET-TS EAMCET offers admissions to students in the Telangana state institutes on the basis of their scores in the entrance examination.

    • Detailed B.Tech Syllabus

    A Bachelor of Technology is a four-year undergraduate program. This course focuses mainly on the theoretical and practical aspects of the application in streams like Computer Science Engineering, Civil Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, Biochemical Engineering, Electrical and Electronics Engineering, Robotics and Automation Engineering, Automobile Engineering, Food Technology, etc. A broad range of specialisations offered in the BTech program enables the students to choose the desired course based on their area of interest. This course requires quick learning and innovative skills.
    Students looking forward to pursuing their careers in the fields mentioned earlier could take up this course.

    BTech Syllabus: Important Facts

    • 1.This is a four-year course divided into 8 semesters. Each semester has the required number of subjects, and the credits are awarded accordingly.

    • 2.As this course has various specialisations, each specialisation offers multiple subjects, out of which a few subjects might be interrelated to the other specialisation. Subjects like Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, English, Mechanics, Electrical Technology, Programming, Engineering Drawing and Graphics, Data Structures, etc., are common subjects in the BTech curriculum.

    • 3.These subjects enable the students to have an in-depth analysis and understanding of technical terms and the theoretical and practical aspects of each subject related to engineering, which is applied in real life. A lot of innovation and self-equipped skills would be essential in the duration of this course.

    • 4.Each student should obtain a minimum credit score to complete this course. There would also be a project that each student should undertake by the beginning of the fourth year. The duration and type of the project, along with its credit score, varies from one college/university to the other. For instance, the final-year project in the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) could be carried out within the department or outside the department of IIT. The credit varies based on the selection of the project concerning the department in the same college or university.

    • 5.Apart from the core subjects, students can take elective courses based on their choice and interests. These elective courses can be chosen at the students' discretion during any semester. These elective courses vary from one college/university to the other.


    Syllabus for Distance Program in BTech

    As per the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), distance education is not allowed for the engineering stream. They have implemented an option of distance education for working professionals. These correspondence courses are well suited for working professionals.

    The syllabus would be the same as the BTech regular programme. However, top colleges do not provide a distance education programme for BTech.

    Other insights: BTech through distance education has to be completed within a minimum of 4 years and a maximum of 6 years. Students would get access to the study materials both offline and online, and they could also attend lectures through video conferencing. The distance BTech course enables the students to gain insights on developing their professional potential and technical ability and aims to bridge India's technological gap. There would be two exams each year, out of which one happens in June and the other in December. The final grading process would be such that 70% of the grades obtained in the semester exams would be considered.


    List of BTech Subjects

    The core subjects in each specialisation are mostly covered between the first and the fourth semesters. The core subjects include Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Engineering Sciences, English, Mechanics, Electrical Technology, Programming, Engineering Drawing and Graphics, Data Structures and Technical Arts. The remaining four semesters cover a variety of professional and elective courses, which include Humanities, Advanced Digital System Design, Advanced Coding Theory, and Social Sciences. Laboratories and Project Seminars also form part of this course. The electives are open to students. They can choose the desired elective from any department in the college/institute.

    BTech Admission Process

    All the BTech aspirants need to follow a similar process for admission to any institute. Various stages for BTech admissions are given below:

    • • Firstly, BTech aspirants need to appear and qualify for any of the entrance exams for admission to an institute.

    • • National level engineering entrance exams such as JEE Main is accepted by NITs, IIITs, GFTIs and several other private colleges. JEE Advanced is the entrance exam conducted for admission to IITs.

    • • Other than the aforesaid two exams, B Tech Admissions are also conducted through state-level exams such as WBJEE, KEAM, AP EAMCET, TS EAMCET, MHT CET etc. and university/college level exams such as BITSAT, VITEEE, SRMJEEE, UPESEAT, etc.

    • • All the engineering entrance exams are conducted in April, May and June every year.

    • • After the result declaration of every exam, counselling and seat allotment process has been conducted by the concerned authorities. During counselling, students have to choose college and courses of their choice based on their ranks.

    • • After seat allotment, every student needs to visit the allotted engineering college to complete the admission formalities.

    Questions & Answers

    BE/BTech Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) :-

    Q. What is the basic difference between Bachelor of Technology and Bachelor of Engineering?

    A. Bachelor of Engineering or BE course is more theoretical in nature, whereas, BTech courses are more practical oriented. The key difference between these two degree programmes is with respect to the course material taught and the orientation of the programme.

    Q. What can I do with a Bachelor of Technology or BTech?

    A. Candidates can make a career in different fields after graduating in BTech, such as Software Developer, Information Technology Consultant, Computer Forensic Analyst, Information Technology Business Analyst and Computer Network Architect.

    Q. What are the skills required for BE/BTech?

    A. Candidates are required to have the below skills to perform well in their BE/BTech course. Have a look:

    • Ability to communicate - verbal and written

    • Familiarity with Industry Standards

    • Knowledge of the core subject

    • Teamwork

    • Creativity

    • Multi-disciplinary exposure

    • Application of the knowledge

    • Leadership Skills

    • Analytical & Creative thinking

    • Acquaintance with Computers & IT

    Q. What is the eligibility criteria for BE/BTech?

    A. Candidates must have passed the Class 12 exam from a recognised board with Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics as core subjects. They must have secured minimum aggregate marks of 60% in the above subjects combined.

    Q. What are the different job profiles offered to a BTech graduate?

    A. BTech graduates are mostly hired in technical fields. Any candidate who has completed a BTech degree can get a job in almost all the sectors in India and they are also hired as consultants, subject matter experts, researchers, managers etc.

    Q. What is the best career option after B Tech in CSE?

    A. A BTech graduate in CSE can choose between numerous career options including Data Analyst, Software developer, Networking Engineer, Database Administrator, Testing Engineer, Game Developer, and Information security specialist.

    Q. What is the scope of B Tech in information technology?

    A. The focus of BTech in IT (Information technology) is on the field of computers and technology. It differs from Computer Science engineering in this aspect because in BTech students are expected to learn management and information theory. It includes a detailed study of programming and of computer software.


    Q. Which branch of BTech has more scope?

    A. The most popular branch to pursue engineering is BTech in Mechanical Engineering followed by computer science, Electrical, and Civil. There are also other engineering branches like Chemical, Biotechnology, Aeronautical, Industrial, Instrumentation, Marine, etc. which have a good scope.

    Q. Is BTech a good choice?

    A. Yes, BTech is a good choice and course for students with creative and technical minds. It also provides you with great career opportunities.

    Q. What is the average salary of a BTech engineer in India?

    A. As a fresher, a BTech graduate can earn an average of Rs 15,000- Rs 30,000 per month. In other cases, the salary of a candidate depends upon the job profile, too. If you work as a Project Engineer, Design Mechanical Engineer you can earn an average of 1.5- 8 lakhs annually.

    Q. What is the full form of BTech? What is the duration of the course?

    A. BTech stands for Bachelor of technology. It is an undergraduate engineering degree awarded to a candidate. The duration of this degree course is four years.

    A Bachelor of Technology is considered as the gateway to the field of engineering. BTech course is a full-time four-year undergraduate-level course that is offered by various engineering colleges/institutes. The 4-year BTech degree course is spread over 8 semesters. The alternative to the BTech course is Bachelor of Engineering. BE is more of a theoretical-based program while a Bachelor of Technology is based on practical learning as well as theoretical learning.
