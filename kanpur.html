IIT Kanpur Admission Process

For BTech and BSc, the admission process is completely conducted by JoSAA after students appear for their JEE Main and JEE Advanced exam. For other courses, such as MTech, MSc, PhD and MDes, students are required to appear for their specific entrance exams and attend exam-specific counselling process for seat allocation.

STEP 1: IIT Kanpur Application Process 2022 (All Courses)

The application process for a few popular courses completely online. Candidates seeking admission must apply via the following websites:

  • BTech and BSc/ BS aspirants must fill out the application form for JEE (Main) through the official website of the National Testing Agency (NTA) (nta.ac.in). The application fee for boys is INR 625 and INR 325 for girls
  • MTech candidates are required to register for GATE through the official website of GATE Online Application Processing System (GOAPS) (appsgate.iitd.ac.in). The application fee for boys is INR 1,500 and INR 750 for girls
  • For MSc, candidates need to register for IIT JAM through the official website (iitr.ac.in). The application fee for others is INR 1,800 for other and INR 900 for female (all categories)/ SC/ ST/ PwD for one test paper
  • The aspirants of MDes need to apply for CEED before submitting an application form at the institute. The candidates also need to submit several supporting documents and an application fee during the application process. The application fee for boys is INR 3,200 and INR 1,600 for girls
  • For MBA and PhD, candidates are required to apply via the official website of IIT Kanpur.

Step 2: Selection Rounds

After applying, the institute considers the merit of the candidates for MBA and PhD admission. For BTech, MDes, MSc, BSc and MTech courses, JEE Advanced, IIT JAM CEED and GATE scores are considered for the selection of candidates. Additionally, admission to PhD and MS by Research is based on UGC NET/ CSIR NET and other national-level exam scores.

Step 3: Merit List/ Cutoff and Admission Confirmation

JoSAA, COAP and CCMN Counselling are conducted for seat allotment to BTech, MTech and MSc courses. A merit list is released by the institute for announcing the names of the candidates shortlisted for certificate, PhD and MS by Research courses followed by a counselling round. Candidates need to pay the admission fee to confirm their admission.

IIT Kanpur - Indian Institute of Technology Scholarships and Funding

Scholarship 1

Merit-cum-Means (MCM) Scholarships:

The Institute gives Merit-cum-Means (MCM) Scholarships to meritorious and needy students. Details and Criteria are given

An undergraduate (UG) student in the 4-year, 5-year, or 2-year M.Sc. program can receive the MCM scholarship subject to the following criteria/conditions:

1) Up to 25% of the students in a batch may be awarded the MCM scholarship. From the total number of MCM Scholarships available in each batch, 20% shall be reserved for students belonging to the SC/ST category. The SC/ST students are not to be compared with the GN/OBC students.
2) The MCM scholarships unutilized in a batch may be transferred to another batch. Similarly, the MCM scholarships unutilized in SC/ST category may be transferred to other category students.
3) A student should have a CPI of 6.5 or above to be eligible to receive a full MCM scholarship (freeship + pocket allowance). If the CPI of a student falls below 6.5 (but remains above 6.0), then the pocket allowance is withdrawn.
4) For the first-year UG students, common JEE-AIR or marks obtained will be used for awarding the MCM scholarship.
5) A student must not have had an 'F' grade(s) in one or more courses in the last two regular semesters to receive the MCM scholarship.
6) Total Family Annual Income (TFAI) of the student from all sources should be less than 6.0 lacs.
br 7) The student must not be getting any other scholarship either from IIT Kanpur or from any other external sources.

Scholarship 2

Inspire Scholarships:

The Department of Science and Technology (DST), Government of India gives Scholarships for Higher Education (SHE), called INSPIRE-SHE Scholarships, to the students enrolled in undergraduate programs in natural and basic sciences all across the country in order to inspire them to become leading scientists.

The details of the INSPIRE Scholarship are provided below:

1) Students pursuing 4-Year BS and 5-Year BS-MS Dual Degree programs in natural and basic sciences AND have secured rank within top 10,000 in JEE-AIR are eligible to apply for INSPIRE Scholarship.
2) Applicants must have secured marks within top 1% of their board
3) Applicants must have secured rank within top 10,000 in JEE-AIR
4) Applicants are KVPY, NTSE, JBNSTS scholar, or International Olympiad medalist
5) As per the DST guidelines, the students pursuing BS program in Economics and B. Tech./Dual-Degree programs in Engineering are NOT eligible to receive INSPIRE Scholarship.
6) If applicants have failed in one or more courses or their CPI is below 7.0, then they are NOT eligible to receive INSPIRE scholarship.
